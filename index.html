About

Eliana Barela is an artist from Tom√©, New Mexico. She specializes in acrylic and oil paintings, as well as photography and woodworking. Her Hispanic culture, family, and faith drive her artistry. She is majoring in Art Education at the University of New Mexico and plans to get her master's in Art Therapy. For the past year, she has worked as a children's painting teacher at a local art center and is also pursuing her dream of becoming a Santera through a mentorship. A Santera is an Spanish Colonial artist who creates Santos y Revultos and other Spanish religious artworks all through natural materials found here in New Mexico!

Awards and Recognition

Eliana's work has been recognized locally as well as Statewide. Best of Show Los Lunas Museum of Heritage and Arts Student Art Show, acrylic painting. 1st place Bosque Farms Fair. 2nd place in the Professional Hispanic Art Gallery Acrylic Oil watercolor painting section in the New Mexico State Fair, oil painting.
